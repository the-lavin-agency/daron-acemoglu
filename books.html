What Happened to
Liberal Democracy?

With James A. Robinson

Liberal democracy is in crisis. Around the world, the institutions that once guaranteed civil liberties, political competition, and accountable governance are under sustained assault — from populist leaders, captured courts, weakened civil societies, and a concentrated media landscape. What Happened to Liberal Democracy? delivers an urgent, evidence-driven diagnosis of democracy's fragility, and a clear-eyed case for what it will take to restore it.

Drawing on the foundational framework of Why Nations Fail and The Narrow Corridor, Acemoglu and Robinson show that liberal democracy is not a stable equilibrium — it is a precarious achievement that must be actively maintained.

Power and Progress

Our Thousand-Year Struggle Over Technology & Prosperity — With Simon Johnson

Technologies have always promised to transform humanity for the better. And yet, throughout history, the benefits of technological progress have rarely flowed automatically to the many — they have instead been captured by the few. Power and Progress traces this pattern across a thousand years, from the medieval agricultural revolution to the Industrial Revolution, from computing to artificial intelligence.

The message is urgent and actionable: the direction of AI is not fixed. But getting it right will require deliberate choices — by governments, companies, and citizens — about who technology should serve.

The Narrow Corridor

States, Societies, and the Fate of Liberty — With James A. Robinson

Why do some societies achieve and sustain liberty while others fall into despotism? In this landmark work, Acemoglu and Robinson argue that liberty emerges only when a capable state and a mobilized society are in balance — and that this balance is far more fragile, and far more rare, than we assume.

Drawing on anthropology, history, and economic theory, The Narrow Corridor reframes the central question of political economy: not just how nations become rich, but how they become — and remain — free.

Why Nations Fail

The Origins of Power, Prosperity, and Poverty — With James A. Robinson

Why are some nations rich and others poor? Economists have proposed many answers — geography, culture, colonial history, natural resources. Acemoglu and Robinson demolish them all, and replace them with a single, evidence-backed framework: inclusive political and economic institutions create prosperity; extractive ones destroy it.

Why Nations Fail became a global phenomenon — a New York Times bestseller translated into over 40 languages, endorsed by Jared Diamond, and described by Nobel Laureate George Akerlof as "a brilliant and uplifting book that advances our understanding of what makes nations succeed or fail." The research underpinning it would ultimately win Acemoglu the Nobel Prize in Economics in 2024.

Academic Research

Selected Publications

With over 1,000 citations across economics' top journals, Daron's academic output spans political economy, labor, growth theory, networks, and the economics of AI.

Economic Policy · 2024

The Simple Macroeconomics of AI

A rigorous, task-based assessment of AI's likely impact on GDP and productivity. Challenges hyper-optimistic forecasts and maps the conditions for genuine broad-based benefits.

Journal of Political Economy · 2019

Democracy Does Cause Growth

With Naidu, Restrepo & Robinson. Establishes causal evidence that democracy promotes long-run economic growth — settling a decades-long debate in political economy.

American Economic Review · 2020

Robots and Jobs: Evidence from US Labor Markets

With Pascual Restrepo. Measures the impact of industrial robots on employment and wages — finds significant negative effects on workers, with concentrated regional harm.

Quarterly Journal of Economics · 2001

The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development

With Johnson & Robinson. The landmark paper establishing how European settler mortality shaped colonial institutions — which in turn determine modern economic development.

Review of Economic Studies · 2002

Reversal of Fortune

With Johnson & Robinson. Shows that once-prosperous pre-colonial civilizations are now relatively poor — evidence that institutions, not geography, drive development outcomes.

NBER Working Paper · 2024

AI, Human Cognition and Knowledge Collapse

Studies how generative and agentic AI shapes human learning incentives and the long-run evolution of society's knowledge — with implications for education, innovation, and human capital.

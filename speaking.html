Signature Topics

What Daron Speaks About

Each talk draws on decades of peer-reviewed research, delivered with clarity and conviction for non-specialist audiences.

AI, Technology & the Future of Work

Is artificial intelligence a tool for shared prosperity — or a mechanism for concentrating wealth and power? Daron's research cuts through the hype to provide a rigorous, evidence-based account of what AI is actually doing to labor markets, wages, and democratic societies. This talk offers a path toward technology that genuinely works for everyone.

Why Nations Fail: Institutions & Prosperity

The fundamental insight at the heart of Daron's Nobel-recognized research: it is the quality of a nation's political and economic institutions — not its geography, culture, or resources — that determines long-run prosperity. This foundational talk draws on historical case studies across centuries and continents.

What Happened to Liberal Democracy?

Democratic institutions around the world are under unprecedented strain. Daron examines the structural forces behind democratic backsliding — elite capture, weakened checks and balances, and the erosion of civil society — and makes the case for why reversing these trends is both urgent and possible.

Power & Progress: A Thousand Years of Technology

Throughout history, transformative technologies have not automatically delivered broad-based benefits. From the printing press to the steam engine to digital computing, Daron traces how the direction of technological change — not its speed — determines who wins and who loses. The lesson for AI is urgent.

Inequality: Causes, Consequences & Policy

Rising inequality is not an inevitable byproduct of economic growth — it is the result of specific policy choices, technological trajectories, and institutional arrangements. Daron's framework identifies the levers most likely to produce lasting, equitable change.

The Narrow Corridor: Freedom vs. State Power

Liberty is not the default condition of societies — it requires a delicate balance between a capable state and a mobilized society. Daron's research identifies the narrow corridor in which democracy and individual freedom can be sustained, and explains why so few societies manage to stay within it.
